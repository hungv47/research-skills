# Target Stress-Test Questions

Validation questions to pressure-test targets before committing. Weak targets fail these questions. Strong targets have clear answers.

---

## Universal Questions

Apply these to every Key Result:

### 1. The Revenue Test
> "If we achieve this but revenue doesn't improve, was it worth it?"

**What it catches:** Vanity metrics, disconnected goals
**Good answer:** Clear path from metric to revenue
**Bad answer:** "It's about brand awareness" (with no conversion path)

### 2. The Falsifiability Test
> "What evidence would prove this target is wrong?"

**What it catches:** Unfalsifiable goals, sandbags
**Good answer:** Specific data that would indicate failure
**Bad answer:** "Nothing, this is definitely the right target"

### 3. The Ownership Test
> "Who specifically owns this? What are they NOT doing to focus on it?"

**What it catches:** Orphan targets, lack of trade-offs
**Good answer:** Named person, deprioritized work listed
**Bad answer:** "The team owns it" or "They'll fit it in"

### 4. The Measurement Test
> "Can we measure this weekly, or will we only know at quarter end?"

**What it catches:** Lagging indicator blindness
**Good answer:** Weekly leading indicators identified
**Bad answer:** "We'll count at the end of the quarter"

### 5. The 70% Test
> "If we hit 70% of this target, is that still valuable?"

**What it catches:** All-or-nothing goals, binary outcomes
**Good answer:** "70% would be significant progress"
**Bad answer:** "If we don't hit 100%, we failed"

### 6. The Accountability Test
> "If we miss this, what happens? Who's accountable?"

**What it catches:** Consequence-free targets
**Good answer:** Clear consequences, named owner
**Bad answer:** "We'll adjust and try again"

### 7. The Connection Test
> "How does this contribute to [company's #1 priority]?"

**What it catches:** Island Effect, misaligned targets
**Good answer:** Direct, traceable connection
**Bad answer:** "It's a different priority"

## Stage-Specific Questions

### Awareness Stage Targets

> "Are we measuring reach or resonance?"

**Good answer:** Targeting qualified reach, not raw impressions
**Bad answer:** "More eyeballs is always better"

> "What happens after someone sees this? What's the next step?"

**Good answer:** Clear funnel to consideration
**Bad answer:** "They'll remember us"

> "How do we know these are the right people?"

**Good answer:** Defined ICP, targeting criteria
**Bad answer:** "Anyone who might be interested"

### Acquisition Stage Targets

> "What's the CAC for this channel? Is it sustainable?"

**Good answer:** Known CAC, within target range
**Bad answer:** "We'll figure that out later"

> "If we 10x this channel, does quality hold?"

**Good answer:** Tested scalability
**Bad answer:** "Probably"

> "Are we acquiring users or acquiring revenue?"

**Good answer:** Clear path to monetization
**Bad answer:** "Users first, revenue later"

### Activation Stage Targets

> "What's the 'aha moment' and how do we know users hit it?"

**Good answer:** Defined activation event, tracked
**Bad answer:** "When they sign up"

> "How long should activation take? What's blocking it?"

**Good answer:** Target TTV, known friction points
**Bad answer:** "As fast as possible"

> "What % of users never activate? Why?"

**Good answer:** Segment analysis, drop-off reasons
**Bad answer:** "We don't track that yet"

### Retention Stage Targets

> "What's the natural usage frequency for this product?"

**Good answer:** Defined cadence (daily/weekly/monthly)
**Bad answer:** "As often as possible"

> "Why do users leave? What's the #1 churn reason?"

**Good answer:** Exit survey data, cohort analysis
**Bad answer:** "Various reasons"

> "What's the leading indicator of churn?"

**Good answer:** Early warning signal identified
**Bad answer:** "We find out when they cancel"

### Revenue Stage Targets

> "Is this expansion from happy customers or desperate upselling?"

**Good answer:** NPS correlation, organic expansion
**Bad answer:** "Sales is pushing upgrades"

> "What's the LTV:CAC for each segment?"

**Good answer:** Segment-level unit economics
**Bad answer:** "Blended average only"

> "Is revenue sustainable or one-time?"

**Good answer:** Recurring revenue focus, predictable
**Bad answer:** "Big deals whenever we can"

### Referral Stage Targets

> "What makes someone refer? What's the trigger?"

**Good answer:** Identified referral moments
**Bad answer:** "If they like us, they'll tell friends"

> "What's the incentive? Does it attract quality referrals?"

**Good answer:** Aligned incentives, quality over quantity
**Bad answer:** "Gift cards for everyone referred"

> "What's the viral coefficient? Is referral accretive or just marketing?"

**Good answer:** K-factor calculated, >0.5
**Bad answer:** "People share sometimes"

## Team-Specific Questions

### For Engineering Targets

> "Is this measuring output (features) or outcome (impact)?"

**What to avoid:** "Ship X" instead of "X improves Y"

> "Are we measuring engineering health? (velocity, quality, debt)"

**What to include:** Balance delivery with sustainability

> "What's the cost of NOT doing this? What breaks?"

**Why ask:** Prioritization requires understanding consequences

### For Product Targets

> "How will we know users want this vs. we think they need this?"

**What to avoid:** Building without validation

> "What's the smallest experiment to test this hypothesis?"

**Why ask:** Avoid over-investment in unvalidated bets

> "If users don't adopt this, what will we learn?"

**Why ask:** Failure should still produce insight

### For Marketing Targets

> "Are we measuring activities or pipeline contribution?"

**What to avoid:** Content calendar completion as success

> "What's the attribution model? How confident are we?"

**Why ask:** Marketing measurement is notoriously fuzzy

> "If budget was cut 50%, which activities would remain?"

**Why ask:** Force prioritization of highest-impact work

### For Sales Targets

> "Are these quotas or stretch goals?"

**What to watch:** Targets shouldn't just be comp-plan targets

> "What's the quality bar for new deals?"

**Why ask:** Avoid bad-fit customers that churn

> "Is the pipeline realistic or aspirational?"

**Why ask:** Bottom-up vs top-down target setting

## Red Flag Responses

When stress-testing reveals these answers, push back:

| Response | Red Flag | Follow-up |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| "It's hard to measure" | Unmeasurable KR | "Then how will we know if we succeeded?" |
| "Leadership wants this" | No ownership | "Who's accountable for making it happen?" |
| "We've always done this" | Zombie target | "What would change if we dropped it?" |
| "It depends" | Unclear target | "On what specifically?" |
| "Trust me" | No evidence | "What data supports this target?" |
| "We'll figure it out" | No plan | "What's the first step this week?" |
| "It's obvious" | Untested assumption | "When did we last validate this?" |

## The Final Gut Check

Before finalizing any target, ask:

> "If we're at 50% of this target in month two, what would we do?"

**Good answer:** Specific interventions planned
**Bad answer:** "Work harder"

> "What would have to be true for this to fail even if we execute well?"

**Good answer:** External dependencies, assumptions identified
**Bad answer:** "Nothing, it's all up to us"

> "In 90 days, will we regret setting this target?"

**Good answer:** "It's ambitious but right"
**Bad answer:** "Maybe it's too easy/hard but let's see"
